# Clinical Letter Template: Employment Support

## For Mental Health Professionals Supporting Client Employment Applications

---

## Instructions for Clinicians

**When to use this letter:**
- Client has been rejected from employment due to background check/screening
- Client has a documented mental health condition that contributed to adverse history
- Client is requesting reasonable accommodation or reconsideration

**Important considerations:**
- Obtain written consent before sending any clinical information
- Only disclose minimum necessary information
- Do not guarantee outcomes to client
- Document that letter was provided in clinical record

---

## TEMPLATE A: General Employment Support Letter

[Practice Letterhead]

[Date]

[Employer Name]
[Company Name]
[Address]

RE: [Client Name] - Employment Consideration

Dear Hiring Manager / Human Resources:

I am writing in my capacity as [Client Name]'s [therapist/counselor/case manager] to provide context regarding their employment history that may have appeared in background screening.

**Clinical Context:**

[Client Name] has been under my care since [date]. During [time period], they experienced [general description - e.g., "a significant health crisis," "circumstances that temporarily affected their employment stability," "challenges that have since been addressed through treatment"].

**Current Status:**

I can attest that [Client Name] is currently:
- Engaged in regular treatment and demonstrating consistent progress
- Stable and capable of maintaining employment responsibilities
- Motivated and committed to professional success
- [Add other relevant positive observations]

**Professional Opinion:**

Based on my clinical assessment, the circumstances that led to [the employment gap / the incident in question / the adverse history] do not reflect [Client Name]'s current capabilities or character. [He/She/They] have demonstrated significant growth and have developed effective strategies for maintaining stability.

**Recommendation:**

I respectfully request that [Client Name] be given fair consideration for employment. I am confident that [he/she/they] will be a reliable and dedicated employee.

If you have questions or require additional information, please contact me with the client's written authorization.

Sincerely,

## Documentation Checklist

Before sending any clinical letter:

- [ ] Written consent obtained from client (HIPAA authorization)
- [ ] Client understands what will and won't be disclosed
- [ ] Letter reviewed with client before sending
- [ ] Only minimum necessary information included
- [ ] No specific diagnoses unless explicitly authorized and necessary
- [ ] Copy placed in clinical record
- [ ] Client informed that letter does not guarantee outcome

---

## Important Notes

1. **Scope of Practice:** Clinical letters provide context and professional opinion. They are not legal documents and do not override employer policies.

2. **HIPAA Compliance:** Always obtain written authorization before disclosing any clinical information. Use minimum necessary standard.

3. **Managing Expectations:** Be clear with clients that letters may help but do not guarantee employment. Algorithmic systems may not even be reviewed by humans.

4. **Documentation:** Keep copies of all letters in clinical records with consent forms.

5. **Follow-up:** Consider scheduling follow-up to process outcomes, whether positive or negative.
